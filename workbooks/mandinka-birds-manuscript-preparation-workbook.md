# AI West Africa Manuscript Preparation Workbook

## Mandinka Birds for the AIWA Junior Publishing Fellowship

**Publisher Context:** AI West Africa — An African Owned, Busumbala Born, Gambian Grown Company.

## Fellowship Positioning

This workbook prepares the learner to work as a **Linguistic Steward** and **Media Producer** in the AIWA Junior Publishing Fellowship. Every lesson develops material for a future school textbook chapter on Mandinka bird knowledge. Every capture task is treated as a **textbook contribution** under a model of **New Authorship**.

## Lesson 1 Ethics Checkpoint (Mandatory)

Before beginning Lesson 1, complete the UNESCO cultural protocol checklist:

- Confirm elder knowledge holders have granted permission for topic discussion.
- Confirm the speaker community accepts classroom publication of proposed terms.
- Confirm culturally sensitive names, stories, or ritual references are excluded unless explicitly authorized.

## Lesson 1: Authorship Charter for Bird Knowledge

**Objective:** Define your role as a contributor-author.

### New Authorship Activities (Lesson 1)

1. Write a two-paragraph author statement explaining how your chapter will preserve Mandinka ecological vocabulary.
2. Draft a contributor agreement line: “This entry is a textbook contribution for future students, reviewed through community protocol.”
3. Produce a short audio introduction naming yourself as Linguistic Steward and Media Producer.

## Lesson 2 Ethics Checkpoint (Mandatory)

- Verify all bird terms will be credited to recognized community speakers.
- Verify naming variants will be presented without ranking dialects as superior.
- Verify species references are educational and non-extractive.

## Lesson 2: Lexicon Development for Textbook Use

**Objective:** Build a structured Mandinka bird lexicon.

### New Authorship Activities (Lesson 2)

1. Create a table with Mandinka bird term, English gloss, habitat note, and pronunciation guidance.
2. Record two sample sentences per term for textbook sidebars.
3. Mark each row as “Approved textbook contribution” only after peer review.

## Lesson 3 Ethics Checkpoint (Mandatory)

- Confirm image or sound capture occurs only with permission in the location.
- Confirm sacred, private, or restricted spaces are not documented.
- Confirm all media includes contributor metadata for accountability.

## Lesson 3: Field Media for School Publication

**Objective:** Produce educational media assets for chapter integration.

### New Authorship Activities (Lesson 3)

1. Produce one bird-call recording and one habitat image per selected term.
2. Write a caption template connecting media evidence to Mandinka terminology.
3. Submit each item as a “future textbook contribution,” not as raw data.

## Lesson 4 Ethics Checkpoint (Mandatory)

- Confirm intergenerational review includes at least one elder and one youth reviewer.
- Confirm corrections are logged transparently.
- Confirm unresolved disputes are flagged for editorial hold.

## Lesson 4: Editorial Review and Manuscript Assembly

**Objective:** Convert contributions into chapter-ready manuscript pages.

### New Authorship Activities (Lesson 4)

1. Assemble a draft chapter sequence: introduction, lexicon, usage examples, media plates.
2. Add attribution lines for each contribution, including reviewer names or roles.
3. Prepare a final “Community Review Notes” block for textbook editors.

## Completion Deliverable

Submit a manuscript packet titled **Mandinka Birds: Community Authored Learning Chapter** containing:

- Authorship charter
- Ethics checkpoint records
- Lexicon pages
- Captioned media contributions
- Editorial review log
